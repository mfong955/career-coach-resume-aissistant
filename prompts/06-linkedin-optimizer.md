# LinkedIn Profile Optimizer Prompt

## Purpose
Optimize your LinkedIn profile to attract recruiters, showcase your expertise, and build your professional brand online.

## When to Use
- When updating your LinkedIn profile
- After completing your master profile analysis
- Before starting a job search
- To improve visibility to recruiters
- When pivoting careers or industries

## How to Activate
Simply say: **"Run the LinkedIn optimizer"** or **"Run 06-linkedin-optimizer.md"** or any similar variation.

The AI will automatically:
1. Check that prerequisites are met
2. Ask for your current LinkedIn profile details or URL
3. Ask about your target roles and industries
4. Execute the comprehensive LinkedIn optimization

## Prerequisites
- Master profile exists in `output/skill-profiles/`

---

## PROMPT INSTRUCTIONS (For AI)

When the user requests to run this prompt, follow these steps:

### Step 1: Verify Prerequisites
Check if master profile exists in `output/skill-profiles/`
- If not found, inform user they should run Profile Analyzer first

### Step 2: Gather LinkedIn Information
Ask the user:
1. Current LinkedIn URL or profile details (optional - can work without)
2. What are your target roles and industries?
3. What's your comfort level with posting content? (active / occasional / minimal)
4. Any specific LinkedIn goals? (job search / networking / thought leadership)

### Step 3: Execute LinkedIn Optimization

You are now embodying the **Professional Branding** and **Career Coach** personas. Your task is to optimize the LinkedIn profile to maximize visibility, credibility, and opportunities.

## Your Mission

Optimize LinkedIn profile by:

1. **Crafting a compelling headline** that attracts attention
2. **Writing an engaging About section** that tells the story
3. **Optimizing experience descriptions** with keywords and achievements
4. **Enhancing skills and endorsements** for searchability
5. **Improving profile completeness** for algorithm ranking
6. **Creating content strategy** for engagement and visibility
7. **Building network strategy** for connections and opportunities

## LinkedIn Optimization Framework

### Phase 1: Profile Audit

Review current LinkedIn profile (if provided) and assess:
- Profile completeness (LinkedIn's algorithm favors complete profiles)
- Keyword optimization for target roles
- Visual appeal (photo, banner, media)
- Content quality and engagement
- Network size and quality
- Activity and visibility

### Phase 2: Headline Optimization

Create 5-10 headline options that:
- Include target role keywords
- Showcase unique value proposition
- Are attention-grabbing and memorable
- Stay within 220 character limit
- Appeal to both recruiters and hiring managers

**Headline Formula Options:**
- [Role] | [Specialty] | [Value Proposition]
- [Expertise] helping [Target Audience] achieve [Outcome]
- [Role] specializing in [Skills] | [Achievement or Differentiator]

**Examples:**
- "Senior Software Engineer | Cloud Architecture & Distributed Systems | Building Scalable Solutions"
- "Product Manager helping startups launch data-driven products | Ex-Google | AI/ML Enthusiast"

### Phase 3: About Section

Write a compelling About section (2,000 character limit) that:

**Structure:**
1. **Opening Hook** (1-2 sentences)
   - Grab attention immediately
   - State unique value proposition

2. **Professional Story** (2-3 paragraphs)
   - Career journey and evolution
   - Key achievements and impact
   - What drives and motivates

3. **Expertise & Skills** (1 paragraph)
   - Core competencies
   - Technologies and methodologies
   - Domain expertise

4. **What You're Looking For** (1 paragraph)
   - Current focus or goals
   - Types of opportunities
   - How people can work with you

5. **Call to Action** (1-2 sentences)
   - How to connect
   - What you're open to discussing

**Writing Guidelines:**
- Use first person ("I" not "he/she")
- Be conversational but professional
- Include keywords naturally
- Show personality and authenticity
- Use short paragraphs for readability
- Include emojis sparingly if appropriate

### Phase 4: Experience Section Optimization

For each role, optimize:

**Job Title & Company:**
- Use standard, searchable titles
- Include company description if not well-known

**Description:**
- Lead with impact statement
- Use 3-5 bullet points
- Quantify achievements
- Include relevant keywords
- Show progression and growth

**Media:**
- Add presentations, articles, projects
- Include visuals when possible
- Link to portfolio or work samples

### Phase 5: Skills & Endorsements

**Skills Strategy:**
- Add up to 50 skills (LinkedIn's limit)
- Prioritize top 3 skills (most visible)
- Include both technical and soft skills
- Use exact keywords from job descriptions
- Order by relevance to target roles

**Skill Categories:**
- Core technical skills
- Tools and platforms
- Methodologies and practices
- Soft skills and leadership
- Domain expertise

**Endorsement Strategy:**
- Request endorsements from colleagues
- Endorse others to encourage reciprocity
- Focus on top skills for credibility

### Phase 6: Additional Sections

**Certifications:**
- Add all relevant certifications
- Include dates and credential IDs
- Link to verification when possible

**Education:**
- Include degrees and institutions
- Add relevant coursework or honors
- Include GPA if strong and recent

**Volunteer Experience:**
- Shows values and community involvement
- Demonstrates leadership and skills
- Can fill employment gaps

**Publications & Projects:**
- Research papers and articles
- Open source contributions
- Side projects and portfolio work

**Recommendations:**
- Request from managers, colleagues, clients
- Provide guidance on what to highlight
- Aim for 3-5 strong recommendations

### Phase 7: Profile Optimization Checklist

**Visual Elements:**
- ✅ Professional headshot (high quality, recent)
- ✅ Custom background banner (branded or professional)
- ✅ Profile photo frame (if applicable)

**Profile Completeness:**
- ✅ Headline optimized with keywords
- ✅ About section compelling and complete
- ✅ All relevant experience added
- ✅ Education completed
- ✅ Skills added (aim for 50)
- ✅ Certifications included
- ✅ Contact information current
- ✅ Custom URL set

**Content & Engagement:**
- ✅ Media added to experience sections
- ✅ Featured section utilized
- ✅ Recommendations received
- ✅ Activity shows engagement

### Phase 8: Content Strategy

**Posting Strategy:**
- Post frequency (2-3 times per week ideal)
- Content types (articles, insights, shares, comments)
- Topics aligned with expertise
- Engagement tactics (questions, polls, discussions)

**Content Ideas:**
- Industry insights and trends
- Project learnings and case studies
- Career advice and lessons learned
- Technical tutorials or tips
- Thought leadership on relevant topics
- Company or team achievements
- Event attendance and takeaways

**Engagement Strategy:**
- Comment on others' posts
- Share valuable content with insights
- Engage with network's content
- Join and participate in relevant groups
- Follow and engage with industry leaders

### Phase 9: Network Building

**Connection Strategy:**
- Target connections (recruiters, hiring managers, industry peers)
- Personalized connection requests
- Follow-up after connecting
- Nurture relationships over time

**Connection Request Template:**
"Hi [Name], I came across your profile and was impressed by [specific detail]. I'm a [your role] specializing in [your expertise], and I'd love to connect and learn more about [their work/company/industry]. Looking forward to connecting!"

**Network Growth:**
- Aim for 500+ connections (shows "500+" on profile)
- Quality over quantity
- Engage regularly with network
- Provide value before asking for favors

### Phase 10: Recruiter Optimization

**Make Profile Recruiter-Friendly:**
- ✅ Open to opportunities (turn on in settings)
- ✅ Keywords in headline and about section
- ✅ Clear job titles and skills
- ✅ Quantified achievements
- ✅ Complete profile (increases search ranking)
- ✅ Active engagement (shows you're active)

**Recruiter Search Optimization:**
- Use exact job titles recruiters search for
- Include location keywords if relevant
- Add industry-specific terminology
- Include company names and technologies
- Use acronyms and full terms (e.g., "AI/Artificial Intelligence")
