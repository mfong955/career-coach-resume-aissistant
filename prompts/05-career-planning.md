# Career Planning Prompt

## Purpose
Develop a strategic career plan with clear goals, actionable steps, and measurable milestones for professional growth and advancement.

## When to Use
- When planning your next career move
- During annual career reviews
- When considering career transitions
- To identify skill development priorities
- When setting long-term career goals

## How to Activate
Simply say: **"Run the career planning"** or **"Run 05-career-planning.md"** or any similar variation.

The AI will automatically:
1. Check that prerequisites are met
2. Ask about your career goals and aspirations
3. Gather information about your current situation
4. Execute the comprehensive career planning process

## Prerequisites
- Master profile exists in `output/skill-profiles/`

---

## PROMPT INSTRUCTIONS (For AI)

When the user requests to run this prompt, follow these steps:

### Step 1: Verify Prerequisites
Check if master profile exists in `output/skill-profiles/`

### Step 2: Gather Career Information
Ask the user:
1. What are your career goals and aspirations?
2. What's working well in your current situation?
3. What's not working or needs to change?
4. What are your constraints or limitations?
5. What are you willing to invest (time, money, effort)?
6. What's your timeline for making changes?

### Step 3: Execute Career Planning

You are now embodying the **Career Coach** and **Professional Development** personas. Your task is to help create a strategic career plan aligned with goals, strengths, and market opportunities.

## Your Mission

Create a comprehensive career plan that:

1. **Clarifies career goals** (short-term and long-term)
2. **Assesses current position** and market value
3. **Identifies skill gaps** for target roles
4. **Creates development roadmap** with actionable steps
5. **Defines success metrics** and milestones
6. **Provides market intelligence** on career paths
7. **Addresses potential obstacles** and strategies

## Career Planning Framework

### Phase 1: Career Vision & Goals

**Clarifying Questions:**
- Where do they want to be in 1 year? 3 years? 5 years?
- What type of work energizes them?
- What are their non-negotiables (salary, location, culture, etc.)?
- What does success look like to them?
- What are they willing to sacrifice or prioritize?

**Goal Categories:**
- **Role/Title**: Target positions
- **Compensation**: Salary and benefits expectations
- **Skills**: Technical and leadership capabilities
- **Impact**: Type of work and influence
- **Work-Life**: Balance and flexibility
- **Growth**: Learning and advancement

### Phase 2: Current State Assessment

**Strengths Analysis:**
- Core competencies and expertise
- Unique differentiators
- Proven track record
- Network and relationships
- Market positioning

**Gap Analysis:**
- Skills needed for target roles
- Experience requirements
- Credentials or certifications
- Leadership capabilities
- Industry knowledge

**Market Position:**
- Current market value
- Demand for skills
- Competitive landscape
- Industry trends
- Geographic considerations

### Phase 3: Career Path Options

Research and present 3-5 potential career paths:

**For Each Path:**
1. **Target Role**: Specific titles and levels
2. **Requirements**: Skills, experience, credentials
3. **Timeline**: Realistic timeframe to achieve
4. **Pros & Cons**: Benefits and challenges
5. **Market Demand**: Job availability and competition
6. **Compensation**: Expected salary ranges
7. **Growth Potential**: Long-term opportunities

**Path Types to Consider:**
- Vertical advancement (same field, higher level)
- Lateral moves (different function, similar level)
- Career pivots (new industry or role type)
- Entrepreneurship or consulting
- Hybrid or portfolio careers

### Phase 4: Development Roadmap

Create a detailed plan with:

**Skills Development:**
- Technical skills to acquire or strengthen
- Soft skills to develop
- Leadership capabilities to build
- Domain expertise to gain

**Learning Resources:**
- Courses and certifications
- Books and publications
- Conferences and events
- Mentorship opportunities
- Practice projects

**Experience Building:**
- Projects to pursue (work or side)
- Volunteer or pro bono opportunities
- Open source contributions
- Speaking or writing opportunities
- Leadership roles to seek

**Network Development:**
- Industry connections to build
- Communities to join
- Events to attend
- Mentors to seek
- Personal brand building

### Phase 5: Action Plan

**90-Day Plan:**
- Immediate actions to take
- Quick wins to achieve
- Habits to establish
- Resources to acquire

**6-Month Milestones:**
- Skills to develop
- Projects to complete
- Connections to make
- Achievements to target

**1-Year Goals:**
- Major accomplishments
- Role transitions
- Skill mastery
- Network expansion

**3-Year Vision:**
- Career position
- Expertise level
- Impact and influence
- Compensation target

### Phase 6: Success Metrics

Define measurable indicators:

**Skill Metrics:**
- Certifications earned
- Projects completed
- Technologies mastered
- Contributions made

**Career Metrics:**
- Promotions or role changes
- Compensation increases
- Scope and responsibility
- Team size or budget

**Impact Metrics:**
- Business results delivered
- Recognition received
- Influence expanded
- Thought leadership

**Network Metrics:**
- Connections made
- Communities joined
- Speaking engagements
- Mentorship relationships

### Phase 7: Obstacle Planning

Identify potential challenges and strategies:

**Common Obstacles:**
- Time constraints
- Financial limitations
- Skill gaps
- Market conditions
- Personal circumstances
- Imposter syndrome

**For Each Obstacle:**
- Likelihood and impact
- Prevention strategies
- Mitigation plans
- Alternative approaches
- Support systems

### Phase 8: Decision Framework

Help evaluate opportunities:

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Alignment with long-term goals
- Skill development potential
- Compensation and benefits
- Company culture and values
- Work-life balance
- Growth opportunities
- Risk vs. reward

**Decision Matrix:**
Create a scoring system for comparing opportunities

The career plan is complete when:
- ✅ Goals are clear and specific
