## Resume Writing Principles

### Content Hierarchy
1. **Most Recent & Relevant First**: Reverse chronological order
2. **Impact Over Duties**: What you achieved, not just what you did
3. **Quantify Everything**: Numbers, percentages, dollar amounts
4. **Relevance Rules**: Emphasize what matters for target role
5. **Concise & Powerful**: Every word earns its place

### Achievement Formula
**Action Verb + Task + Method + Result**

Examples:
- "Led team of 5 engineers to deliver project 2 weeks ahead of schedule, reducing costs by $50K"
- "Improved system performance by 40%, reducing latency from 200ms to 120ms"
- "Increased user engagement by 25% through data-driven feature redesign"

### Action Verbs by Category

**Leadership**: Led, Directed, Managed, Coordinated, Mentored, Supervised, Guided

**Achievement**: Achieved, Delivered, Exceeded, Improved, Optimized, Increased, Reduced

**Creation**: Developed, Designed, Built, Created, Implemented, Established, Launched

**Analysis**: Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed, Researched, Identified, Investigated

**Collaboration**: Collaborated, Partnered, Facilitated, Coordinated, Liaised

**Problem-Solving**: Resolved, Troubleshot, Debugged, Fixed, Streamlined, Simplified

## Resume Structure

### Standard Sections (in order)

1. **Contact Information**
   - Name (larger font)
   - Location (city, state)
   - Phone number
   - Email address
   - LinkedIn URL
   - Portfolio/GitHub (if relevant)

2. **Professional Summary** (optional but recommended)
   - 2-3 sentences
   - Key skills and experience
   - Value proposition
   - Tailored to target role

3. **Technical Skills** (for technical roles)
   - Organized by category
   - Prioritize relevant skills
   - Include proficiency levels if helpful

4. **Professional Experience**
   - Reverse chronological
   - Company, title, dates, location
   - 3-5 bullet points per role
   - Focus on achievements and impact

5. **Education**
   - Degree, institution, date
   - Relevant coursework (if recent grad)
   - GPA (if strong and recent)

6. **Certifications** (if applicable)
   - Relevant professional certifications
   - Dates and issuing organizations

7. **Additional Sections** (as relevant)
   - Publications & Research
   - Open Source Contributions
   - Speaking Engagements
   - Awards & Recognition
   - Professional Affiliations
   - Languages

### Formatting Best Practices

**Length**:
- 1 page for <10 years experience
- 2 pages for 10+ years experience
- Never more than 2 pages

**Font**:
- 10-12pt for body text
- 14-16pt for name
- Professional fonts: Arial, Calibri, Garamond, Georgia

**Spacing**:
- Adequate white space
- Consistent margins (0.5-1 inch)
- Clear section breaks
- Easy to scan

**Formatting**:
- Bold for emphasis (company names, titles)
- Consistent date format (MM/YYYY)
- Bullet points, not paragraphs
- No tables, text boxes, or graphics (ATS issues)
- No headers/footers (ATS issues)

## ATS Optimization Strategies

### Keyword Integration
- Extract keywords from job description
- Use exact phrases when appropriate
- Include both acronyms and full terms (e.g., "AI/Artificial Intelligence")
- Integrate naturally into content
- Don't keyword stuff

### Standard Formatting
- Use standard section headings (Experience, Education, Skills)
- Avoid creative section names
- Use simple bullet points
- No tables or columns
- No images or graphics

### File Format
- Submit as .docx or PDF (check job posting)
- Use standard file naming: FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf
- Ensure text is selectable (not image-based)

## Tailoring Process

### For Each Job Application

1. **Analyze Job Description**
   - Identify required vs. preferred qualifications
   - Extract key skills and keywords
   - Understand role priorities
   - Note company culture indicators

2. **Review Candidate Profile**
   - Identify relevant experience
   - Find matching achievements
   - Assess skill alignment
   - Determine positioning strategy

3. **Customize Content**
   - Reorder experience by relevance
   - Emphasize matching achievements
   - Integrate job description keywords
   - Adjust professional summary
   - Prioritize relevant skills

4. **Optimize for ATS**
   - Include exact keyword matches
   - Use standard terminology
   - Ensure clean formatting
   - Verify readability

5. **Quality Check**
   - Verify all facts are accurate
   - Check for errors
   - Ensure consistency
   - Confirm appropriate length

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Errors
- ❌ Listing duties instead of achievements
- ❌ Using passive voice
- ❌ Including irrelevant information
- ❌ Lacking quantifiable results
- ❌ Generic, could-apply-to-anyone content
- ❌ Typos and grammatical errors
- ❌ Inconsistent verb tenses

### Formatting Errors
- ❌ Too long or too short
- ❌ Inconsistent formatting
- ❌ Hard to scan or read
- ❌ ATS-unfriendly elements
- ❌ Unprofessional email address
- ❌ Including photo (US resumes)
- ❌ Personal information (age, marital status)

### Strategic Errors
- ❌ Not tailored to job
- ❌ Missing keywords
- ❌ Wrong emphasis
- ❌ Gaps not addressed
- ❌ Overqualification concerns not handled
